Comparing Two Output Distributions

Study Overview

Large Language Models (LLMs) are stochastic, meaning they don't produce the same output every time. When you change a prompt, the distribution of possible outputs changes too. Rather than judging based on a single example, we need to consider the full range of possibilities each prompt can generate.

In this study, you'll use a visualization tool to compare outputs from two different prompts (Prompt 1 and Prompt 2). Your goal is to explore both sets of outputs and reason about what changed between the two prompts.

  • What kinds of responses are most common for each prompt?
  • How much variety exists in each set of outputs?
  • What do the two sets share, and what differs?

You'll explore the interface, compare the two distributions, and answer questions about how they differ.
